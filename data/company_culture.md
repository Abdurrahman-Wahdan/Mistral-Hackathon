# Zephlen: Company Culture, Values, and HR Interview Guidelines (INTERNAL ONLY)
**CONFIDENTIAL: FOR HR AND HIRING MANAGERS ONLY. DO NOT SHARE WITH CANDIDATES.**

## About Zephlen
Based in Istanbul, Turkey, Zephlen is a frontier artificial intelligence company specializing in autonomous, specialized AI agents. We build AI that doesn't just chat, but *acts, plans, and executes* complex workflows across APIs, internal tools, and unstructured web environments. We are currently scaling our core platform and expanding our fleet of vertical-specific agents tailored for the Turkish and global markets.

## Core Values & Culture
Our culture is built around high autonomy, rapid iteration, and deep technical rigor. 

1. **Agentic by Nature:** We expect our employees to be "agentic." If there is a problem, don't just report it; propose a solution and execute it. We value high bias for action.
2. **Pragmatic Imagination:** We are building the future, but we are grounded in engineering reality. We value developers who can dream big but write robust, deterministic, and highly observable code. 
3. **Radical Candor & Low Ego:** The AI landscape moves too fast for politics or ego. We debate ideas vigorously based on merit, not titles. Once a decision is made, we commit.
4. **Resilience to Ambiguity:** There are no runbooks for what we are building. The ideal Zephlen employee thrives when the path is unclear and the technology is uncharted.

## Candidate Expectations for Agentic AI Developer Role

### What We Are Actively Looking For (Green Flags)
*   **System-Level Thinking:** Can they explain how multiple LLM calls interact within a massive autonomous system? Do they understand state management, context windows, and error recovery in agents?
*   **Production Experience:** Have they deployed LLMs/Agents beyond toy Jupyter notebooks? Look for mentions of evaluation frameworks (RAGAS, prompt testing), observability (LangSmith, Phoenix), and CI/CD for ML.
*   **Tool Calling & Grounding:** Deep understanding of function calling, structured outputs (JSON schema, Pydantic), and grounding models with real-world data securely.
*   **True Passion for the Space:** They should be actively reading papers, participating in hackathons, or contributing to open-source agent frameworks (LangChain, LlamaIndex, AutoGen, CrewAI).

### What We Want to Avoid (Red Flags)
*   **The "Prompt Engineer" Trap:** Candidates who think agent development is just writing clever prompts. We need hardcore software engineers who understand infrastructure, concurrency, and API design.
*   **Overcomplicating with Hype:** Candidates who want to use a massive, unproven multi-agent framework for a simple heuristic problem. We value simplicity first.
*   **Inability to Explain Failures:** If they haven't experienced an LLM going off the rails in a loop, hallucinating a critical API call, or dropping context, they haven't worked with agents deeply enough.
*   **"Not My Job" Mentality:** In a startup environment like Zephlen, developers need to be comfortable touching infra, writing evaluations, and talking to users.

## Suggested Interview Questions for HR / First Screen

**1. Cultural Fit (Assessing "Agentic" Behavior):**
*   *Question:* "Tell me about a time you identified a broken process or missing feature and fixed it without being asked. What was the outcome?"
*   *What to look for:* Proactive behavior, end-to-end ownership.

**2. Resilience & Ambiguity:**
*   *Question:* "The field of AI agents changes weekly. Describe a time when a new model release or framework update completely invalidated your recent work. How did you handle it?"
*   *What to look for:* Adaptability, lack of frustration, continuous learning mindset.

**3. High-Level Technical Screen (Non-Coding):**
*   *Question:* "If you were to build an AI agent to automatically review pull requests, what are the biggest risks of it running totally autonomously, and how would you mitigate them?"
*   *What to look for:* Mentions of "human-in-the-loop," sandboxing, hallucination mitigation, cost control, and rate limiting.

## Final Note for Interviewers
At Zephlen, we are not just hiring for what someone knows today, but for their capacity to learn what will be invented tomorrow. Prioritize curiosity, engineering fundamentals, and raw problem-solving speed over specific framework knowledge.
