---
## **4. Final Recommendation**
**Does NOT meet standards** for Interpersonal & Emotional Intelligence. The candidate’s responses reveal **a transactional, low-trust approach to collaboration**, prioritizing personal comfort over team cohesion. Their **inability to manage frustration, lack of empathy, and avoidance of conflict resolution** would likely **erode team morale** and **hinder cross-functional alignment**—critical failures for roles requiring stakeholder management or leadership. Without significant development, they risk **creating silos or toxic dynamics** in high-stakes projects.

---
## **5. Candidate Improvement Actions**
1. **Practice "Nonviolent Communication" (NVC)**:
   - Replace judgmental language ("sorumsuz") with **observations + needs** (e.g., *"I noticed the task wasn’t completed; I need support to meet our deadline. How can we adjust?"*).
   - **Role-play** difficult conversations with a mentor to build empathy.

2. **Develop Conflict Resolution Frameworks**:
   - Learn **structured approaches** (e.g., [Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument](https://kilmanndiagnostics.com/)) to diagnose and address team friction.
   - **Shadow a mediator** in their organization to observe how to de-escalate tensions.

3. **Build Self-Awareness Through Feedback**:
   - Request **360-degree feedback** from peers on their communication style, focusing on **emotional impact** (e.g., *"When I say X, how does it land?"*).
   - Keep an **emotion journal** to track triggers (e.g., "When teammates miss deadlines, I feel Y") and **reframe reactions** (e.g., *"What’s the unmet need here?"*).
