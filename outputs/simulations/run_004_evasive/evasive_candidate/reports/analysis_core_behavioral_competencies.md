## **5. Candidate Improvement Actions**
🔹 **Practice Structured Storytelling for Behavioral Questions**
   - Use the **STAR method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to **explicitly tie challenges to stakeholder dynamics**. Example:
     *"When the client demanded faster delivery at the cost of reliability, I presented a risk matrix showing potential downtime costs (₺X/hour). This shifted the conversation to phased rollouts, saving ₺Y in outages."*

🔹 **Develop Conflict Resolution Examples**
   - Prepare **1–2 concrete stories** about handling pushback (e.g., a teammate resisting a design choice, a user disagreeing with an open-source feature). Focus on:
     - How you **listened to concerns** (e.g., "I held a sync to understand their workflow").
     - How you **aligned on a compromise** (e.g., "We prioritized their use case in the next sprint").

🔹 **Quantify Impact Beyond "It Worked"**
   - Replace vague outcomes ("we fixed it") with **metrics or qualitative results**:
     - *"Reduced API errors by 90% after implementing backoff, avoiding a 2-day delay."*
     - *"The weather integration enabled 15% faster rerouting, which the client highlighted in their Q3 review."*
